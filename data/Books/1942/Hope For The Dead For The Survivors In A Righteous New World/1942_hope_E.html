Her thoughts were interrupted. Rex, the terrier lying on the floor alongside Deborah's rocker, suddenly pricked up his ears, straightened up, gave a sharp woof, and ran to the door. He sniffed at the crack of the threshold. The doorbell rang!

"Your father's late coming home tonight, but Rex doesn't act like that for father. Go see who it is, Deborah."

CAUSE OF MAN'S DEATH

In the beginning Jehovah God created the earth and prepared it for human habitation and then placed Adam upon it. Then he made Eve. As created, they were perfect. In the divine purpose to fill the earth with a race of perfect creatures, God gave to Adam and Eve the divine mandate: "Be fruitful and multiply, and fill the earth, and subdue it." (Genesis 1:28, Roth.) Though not being immortal, those two perfect living human souls, Adam and Eve, could have lived for ever in full health, peace, plenty and happiness, because God made full provision for them whereby their bodies and lives could be sustained in perfection for ever. Their living everlastingly was conditioned on only one thing: they must be perfectly obedient to God. To test their integrity toward him and their worthiness of everlasting life on earth, God forbad them to partake of the tree of knowledge of good and evil and warned them of the penalty of disobedience : "For in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die." (Genesis 2:17) This proves that the human soul is not immortal and also that the penalty for sin is not eternal torment, but death. However, it also proves that the mortal human soul can live on earth for ever under conditions of obedience to and harmony with God, who provides the perfect means to sustain obedient creatures alive.

Yielding to religion, which was introduced by the Devil and which is disobedience to God's will, Adam and Eve broke God's law and thereby sinned. God pronounced sentence upon them and drove them from the garden of Eden, lest they "take also of the tree of life, and eat, and live for ever". (Genesis 3: 22, 23) From then on, the sentenced pair began to die, having lost all right to life. They were deprived of the privilege of fulfilling the divine mandate to fill the earth with a righteous race in God's image and likeness, but God permitted them to reproduce their kind and bring children into the earth. Adam and Eve, being sinners, imperfect, and condemned to death, could not bequeath innocence, perfection and the right to life upon their children. Therefore the apostle writes: "By one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned." (Romans 5:12) By birth or nature they are imperfect and sinners, and hence God cannot approve them and they are condemned and not worthy of everlasting life. Dying, they must all perish, unless God in his mercy and loving-kindness made provision for redemption of men who exercise faith and obedience.

Out of love, and for the vindication of his name, Jehovah God did make such provision. Be it observed that Adam and Eve sinned willfully and at death entered the condition symbolized by "Gehenna", because God's sentence against the willful sinners and rebels can never be recalled, and they are beyond redemption. Adam by his rebellion forfeited perfect life and all right to eternal life, but Jehovah God could make provision that another perfect one should become a man and maintain his integrity toward God under test, and then lay down his earthly human life and thereafter pay over to God the value of that perfect life. Thereby such one could redeem or buy back the liferight and all its attendant blessings and privileges which Adam had forfeited for his offspring. So doing, that one becomes the redeemer or ransomer of those who receive the benefits of his sacrifice. This loving arrangement Jehovah God carried out through his Son, Christ Jesus. "In this was manifested the love of God toward us, because that God sent his only begotten Son into the world, that we might live through him." (1 John 4:9) Christ Jesus laid aside his heavenly glory and his life was transferred to the womb of the virgin and in God's due time the Son of God was born as a man-child. Come to maturity, Jesus consecrated himself to his Father, Jehovah God, to do His will, and God took Jesus into the covenant of sacrifice.

When Jesus symbolized his consecration by being immersed by John the Baptist in Jordan, Jehovah God begot Jesus of the spirit and also anointed him of the spirit and thereby commissioned him to be his Chief Witness to declare Jehovah's name and to bear

testimony to the promised THEOCRATIC GOVERNMENT, to which Christ Jesus was anointed as King. (Matthew 3:13-17) The Devil forthwith sought to bring about the destruction of Christ Jesus and also stirred up the religionists to a wicked opposition to and persecution of Christ Jesus, but under this crucial test Christ Jesus held fast his integrity as Jehovah's servant and witness on earth. Still in his innocence and blamelessness, Christ Jesus was put to death by the Devil's dupes and agents, the religionists, and was buried. But concerning him the prophecy had declared: "Thou wilt not leave my soul in hell; neither wilt thou suffer thine Holy One to see corruption." (Psalm 16:10) On the third day Jehovah God fulfilled this prophecy toward his Son and raised him out of the death state in the grave. Being now "made alive in the spirit", Christ Jesus in due time ascended to heaven and appeared in God's presence and paid over the ransom price in behalf of human creatures that should believe on him and obey him. Concerning this it is written: "Though he were a Son, yet learned he obedience by the things which he suffered; and being made perfect, he became the author of eternal salvation unto all them that obey him." (Hebrews 5:8,9) "For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with [human] hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us.... once in the end

of the world hath he appeared, to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself.... So Christ was once offered to

bear the sins of many; and unto them that look for him shall he appear the second time, without sin unto salvation." -Hebrews 9:24-28.

Salvation, therefore, proceeds from Jehovah and through Christ Jesus. "Salvation belongeth unto Jehovah." (Psalm 3:8, Am. Rev. Ver.) "The wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life, through Jesus Christ our Lord." (Romans 6:23) The benefits of the ransom sacrifice are not for the rebellious wicked, nor did Christ Jesus die for such, because Jehovah God destroys all the wicked. (Psalm 145:20) Christ Jesus tasted death and gave himself a ransom only for all such as believe God and accept his provision for redemption through his beloved Son, and who then devote themselves to God to do his will and who then obey him. Said Christ Jesus: "The Son of man came not to be ministered unto, but to minister, and to give his life a ransom for many." (Matthew 20:28) How many? All those that exercise faith and obey. To this effect it is written: "The Father loveth the Son, and hath given all things into his hand. He that believeth on the Son hath everlasting life: and he that believeth not the Son shall not see life; but the wrath of God abideth on him." (John 3: 35, 36) "He that hath the Son, hath life; and he that hath not the Son of God, hath not life." (1 John 5:12) The unbelievers never come out from under the condemnation, and hence the wrath of God, under which they were born from Adam, continues upon them and they do not get into the way of life. "He that believeth on him is not condemned: but he that believeth not is condemned already, because he hath

not believed in the name of the only begotten Son of God." -John 3:18.

RESURRECTION

Religion, by its teaching of and concerning the immortality of all souls, denies that there are any dead and therefore denies the resurrection of the dead. The Bible differs from religion in that it is the only Book in the world teaching the resurrection hope. On the witness stand the apostle said: "So worship I the God of my fathers, believing all things which are written in the law and the prophets; and have hope toward God, which they themselves also allow, that there shall be a resurrection of the dead, both of the just and unjust." (Acts 24:14,15) Concerning this Jesus said: "The hour cometh, in which all that are in the tombs shall hear his voice, and shall come forth; they that have done good, unto the resurrection of life; and they that have done evil, unto the resurrection of judgment." -John 5:28,29, Am. Rev. Ver.

Resurrection, being God's act by Christ Jesus, means a causing to stand up again, that is, unto life. This proves that the human soul dies and the dead are dead; otherwise there could not be a resurrection of the dead, there being no dead ones to raise to life. The dead ones whom Jesus designates as 'those that have done good' and that come forth from the tomb "unto the resurrection of life" are, first, the Christians who are members of "the body of Christ", and who proved faithful unto death and who are resurrected to life in the spirit, as afore described. They participate with Christ Jesus in the "first resurrection", that is, "first" in time and importance, Christ Jesus himself being "the firstfruits of them that slept". "Jesus Christ, who is the faithful witness, and the first-begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth." (Revelation 1:5) "Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection: on such the second death [or other death, not inherited from the sinner Adam] hath no power, but they shall be priests of God and of Christ, and shall reign with him a thousand years." (Revelation 20:6) The apostle of the Lord also said that at the Lord's coming to the temple for judgment "the dead in Christ shall rise first". (1 Thessalonians 4:16) Those who share in the first resurrection participate with Christ Jesus the King in the heavenly THEOCRATIC GOVERNMENT.

"BETTER RESURRECTION"

The faithful patriarchs, prophets and witnesses who died before Christ's death, resurrection, ascension to heaven and appearance in the presence of God are also included in 'those that have done good' and that come forth "unto the resurrection of life". They did "good", because they devoted themselves to and did the will of God, which is right and good. Those men of old believed God's covenant promise to set up in his appointed time the THEOCRATIC GOVERNMENT by his Messiah, and they devoted themselves unreservedly to Jehovah God and served as his faithful witnesses and kept themselves unspotted from religion and from the Devil's world organization. They endured all manner of persecution therefor, and kept their integrity toward God, faithful unto death, and thus remained on Jehovah's side of the great controversy over UNIVERSAL DOMINATION. Thereby they gained God's approval and the right to a share in the ransom benefits of Christ Jesus. God promises to further reward them with an official position on earth in the service of the heavenly THEOCRATIC GOVERNMENT. Hebrews chapter eleven cites the record of those men of old, from Abel down to and including John the Baptist; and at verse thirty-five he says that they shall have a "better resurrection" than those of humankind who come forth in the general resurrection.

Those faithful witnesses of old did not at death go to heaven. King David was one such, and concerning him the apostle Peter, on the day of Pentecost when the holy spirit was poured out, said by inspiration of that spirit: "David is not ascended into the heavens: but he saith himself, The LORD said unto my Lord, Sit thou on my right hand, until I make thy foes thy footstool." (Acts 2: 34,35) Those faithful men of old will not go to heaven, never having been begotten of God's spirit to life in the spirit. Therefore those faithful Christians who enter into the kingdom of heaven or THEOCRACY are higher and greater than those men of old ever will be. Concerning this Jesus said: "Verily I say unto you, Among them that are born of women there hath not risen a greater than John the Baptist: notwithstanding, he that is least in the kingdom of heaven is greater than he." (Matthew 11:11) Therefore the Christian spiritual class, to whom it is their Father's good pleasure to give the Kingdom, receive a "better thing" than John the Baptist and the other prophets who preceded him: "God having provided some better thing for us [spirit-begotten Christians], that they without us should not be made perfect." -Hebrews 11:40.

In what manner, then, do those faithful men preceding the time of Jesus obtain a "better resurrection"? In this, that, having undergone the test of their integrity faithfully and thus passed a favorable judgment from God, they shall come forth from the graves "unto life" as perfect men, and to be the visible representatives unto mankind of the heavenly THEOCRACY. Under that righteous Government they shall be the princes or governors on the earth. Having been witnesses and prophets before Christ's birth, they were "fathers" respecting him; but being raised from the dead by their Ransomer Christ Jesus, they receive life through him and become his children. Therefore the psalmist addresses Christ Jesus the Theocratic King, saying: "Instead of thy fathers [they] shall be thy children, whom thou mayest make princes in all the earth." (Psalm 45:16) THE THEOCRACY being now established and the King being on his throne, those long-dead men of old may appear among men any day now and shall be seen and recognized by men of faith and good-will, who shall receive instruction through those "princes" among them. (Luke 13: 28, 29) There are a number of scriptures which strongly indicate that the "princes" shall be restored to life immediately before Armageddon and shall witness the destruction of Satan's invisible and visible organization about which they prophesied.

GENERAL RESURRECTION

Concerning those who "have done evil" and who shall come forth "unto the resurrection of judgment", such are "the unjust", in that they have not done the will of God nor passed the test of integrity toward God nor been justified by faith, receiving God's approval. Hence to gain the right to everlasting life on earth these must come forth from the grave and undergo a period of judgment on earth under The THEOCRATIC GOVERNMENT. They must maintain their integrity under test, particularly the test at the end of the thousand-year reign of Christ, when Satan the Devil is loosed out of his restraint for just a little season, that therein he might deceive all those whose heart is not right, after which both he and they shall be destroyed everlastingly as by fire. (Revelation 20:7-10) By the time this general resurrection of the unjust begins the earth's surface will have been brought to a condition of Paradise, the garden of the Lord, through the operation of the Kingdom. Concerning this Jesus spoke to the thief who uttered kind words to him while nailed to the tree and then asked

Jesus to remember him when Jesus should come into his Kingdom. Jesus assured the dying thief that He would remember him and that then in Paradise on earth the thief would be put on judgment and under a test of his integrity to determine whether he would be "with me", that is, in favor of and immovably on the side of the King Christ Jesus. Only by maintaining this position would the once thief gain everlasting life through the King and Redeemer. -Luke 23:42,43.

That kingdom time is the period of regeneration, mentioned by Jesus at Matthew 19:28. During that time those who devote themselves to Jehovah and his King and who obey the Theocratic rule of righteousness shall be regenerated unto life. Continuing faithful, they shall by the King be granted the right to everlasting life on earth under the heavenly THEOCrACY, the "new heavens".

(Jeremiah 25:33) That great time of tribulation was foreshadowed by the flood of Noah's day, and concerning it Jesus said: "Except those days should be shortened, there should no flesh be saved: but for the elect's sake those days shall be shortened." Then the sudden destruction of Armageddon shall come forth upon them, and none of the enemies of Jehovah's THEOCRATIC GOVERNMENT shall escape execution. Read Daniel 11:40 and 1 Thessalonians 5:1-3; also Jeremiah 25:34, 35.

Those having part in the general resurrection of the dead shall neither marry nor be given in marriage, and hence could not be fruitful and reproduce their kind and bring forth children in righteousness and fill the earth with a righteous race. Who, then, shall fulfill that mandate in righteousness and under the righteous "new heavens" and new invisible Overlord, Christ Jesus? Such shall form the "great multitude" that lives and glorifies God and his King on earth for ever. -Revelation 7: 9-17.

(Zephaniah 2:3) Noah's family with him in the ark during the Flood were a type of such Armageddon survivors. Christ Jesus and all the THEOCRACY are the "new heavens". Concerning this the apostle wrote: "We, according to his promise, look for new heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth righteousness." -2 Peter 3:13.

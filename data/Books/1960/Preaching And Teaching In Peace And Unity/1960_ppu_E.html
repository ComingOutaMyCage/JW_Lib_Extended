12SWhen difficulties arise in a congregation between individuals, effort should be made to effect a reconciliation. Judgment of matters affecting the lives of dedicated servants of Jehovah carries with it a great responsibility, and, therefore, the committee should be just and should make sure it has all the facts. The committee must be sure these are witnesses and not gossipers or merely repeating things they have heard. No action can be taken if there is only one witness. If the accused denies the charge throughout, even before the committee, and there is no evidence to prove him guilty, the accuser must at least be put on probation as an apparent liar of a serious kind, and the charge must be held in abeyance until such time as unmistakable proof is brought forth to prove the charged person guilty. If the accused admits the offense later, it would not save him from being disfellowshiped. So, after Matthew 18:15-17 is complied with, the committee would place the confessing one on probation, if repentant, as he would be guilty of either the sin confessed or of lying and bearing false witness against his Christian brother. Probation should serve to help one gain a good conscience before Jehovah. The committee must determine before Jehovah what is to be done. When one is found guilty of committing a wrong and he is disfellowshiped, then a resolution must be drawn up and presented to the congregation, informing the congregation that the individual has been disfellowshiped for conduct unbecoming a Christian.

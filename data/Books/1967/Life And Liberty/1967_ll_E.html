My,

I'm sorry I couldn't talk to you in person because what I wanted to tell you could well affect your entire future. That's why I'm leaving this note.

First, let me ask you this: Isn't life, and the liberty to pursue its pleasures, your most prized possession? How many people do you know who have found only a token of happiness, who really long for contentment and peace of mind? Hasn’t that been so since the beginning of man's imperfect human experience? Yet we seem farther away from it than ever. In fact. I'm sure you'll agree that our own grandparents did not suffer the frustrations that we experience every day. But, you know, our grandparents did something that few families do today. They read the Bible regularly. Do you?

There is a growing tendency to discredit the Bible, to set it aside as of no practical value today. In fact, half the world has thrown the Bible away completely, and it's overrun with communism. But in this other half, the so-called "free world," how many people do you know who really believe the Bible, who solve their problems by heeding its counsel and by following its principles, who are willing to live by its standards? No, the failure is not with the Bible. The failure is the world's—it has failed to heed our Creator's instructions in his Word as to how we should conduct ourselves and conduct our affairs in relation to our fellowmen.

Our relationships to God and to this world and to one another are all important. And they are all interrelated, yet they can conflict one with another. How can we know what is right to do and how can we find the contentment and peace of mind that come only with doing what is right and pleasing to God? This little note cannot possibly say all that you might need to satisfy you completely. So I would like to recommend a book that has really helped me to understand these things from the Bible.
